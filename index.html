What the simulation enables

Four analyses that change how decisions are made

Task Reassignment Delta

Move any stop from one route or day to another. The simulation immediately shows how total distance and time change across every affected route — not just the two you touched. The aggregate delta appears before the change goes live.

→ Per-route delta · Weekly aggregate · Time & distance impact
Route Profitability Analysis

The Twin maps every route against its actual cost — fuel, driver time — and compares it to the revenue or contract value it generates. You see exactly which routes are profitable, which are breaking even, and which are running at a loss.

→ Cost per km · Revenue per stop · Net margin per route
Contract & Tender Pricing

When tendering for a new service contract, the Twin answers the question every ops director asks: how low can we go and still be profitable? Input the proposed service area and frequency — the simulation calculates the operational cost baseline and minimum viable bid.

→ Minimum viable bid · Break-even km · OPEX floor
Capacity Absorption Test

Before buying a new vehicle, test whether your current fleet can absorb new service points through rebalancing. The Twin runs the absorption test and shows the utilization ceiling — often the answer is yes, saving the capital commitment entirely.

→ Utilization rate · Headroom per route · Absorption capacity

The credibility gap

Why ESG commitments without simulation data are a liability

Most carbon reduction commitments in logistics and waste operations are built on projected figures — not simulated outcomes tied to specific operational decisions. When stakeholders, regulators, or auditors ask "how did you arrive at this number?", estimates don't hold up. Simulation data does.

📉
CO₂ Delta Before You Report It

Run the optimised route configuration in the Twin. The simulation calculates the fuel delta, converts it to a CO₂ figure using verified emission factors. You get a projected carbon reduction tied to specific route decisions — not a top-down estimate tied to a target.

⚖️
The Dual Optimisation Proof

The most common executive objection to ESG investment: "it costs more to be green." The Digital Twin runs the What-If that disproves this. Optimise for efficiency and emissions simultaneously — and the simulation shows they're often the same decision.

📋
CSRD & EPR Reporting Support

For organisations under CSRD obligation or EPR frameworks, the Digital Twin provides simulation-backed data that supports Scope 1 emission reporting. Route decisions are linked to emission outcomes — creating an auditable trail from operational change to carbon impact.

🎯
Target-Setting With Evidence

Before committing to a carbon reduction target, simulate it. The Twin shows what operational changes would be required to hit a specific CO₂ figure — so targets are grounded in what your fleet can actually achieve, not what looks good in a report.

Four fleet decisions the simulation answers

Every expansion decision is a hypothesis. The Twin tests it first.

What-If 01
Vehicle ROI Before Purchase

Assign a proposed vehicle to a service zone in the Twin and simulate 52 weeks. Output: projected utilization rate, weekly revenue contribution, operating cost per km, estimated payback period. The boardroom sees a simulation, not a forecast.

What-If 02
Fleet Reduction Test

What if you removed one vehicle? The Twin runs the rebalancing simulation — redistributes its routes to the remaining fleet and shows the utilization impact. Often the result is manageable. Saves the capital cost.

What-If 03
Service Zone Feasibility

Before signing a new service area contract, simulate it. Map the service points, assign routes, stress-test the fleet. The Twin tells you the minimum vehicle and driver count to make the zone viable — before you hire or commit.

What-If 04
Tender Pricing Floor

When bidding on a new contract, the Twin calculates your operational cost floor for the proposed service scope. You know the minimum viable price before you submit — bid competitively without cutting your margin.

Shipment Consolidation

Should these two delivery runs be merged into one? The simulation tests consolidation scenarios and shows the distance and time delta — while checking whether any delivery windows would be breached.

The order in which stops are visited has a measurable distance impact. The Twin tests alternative sequences and finds the shortest path — respecting delivery time windows and vehicle capacity.

Which vehicle should handle this shipment? The simulation tests assignment options — accounting for current load, location, and time constraints — and shows the total cost impact of each choice before dispatch.
